The pattern we see everywhere

Most organisations aren’t failing at strategy. They’re failing at the operating model underneath it.

Across industries and continents, the same pattern repeats. Leaders set bold ambitions on Value. Teams work hard. Budgets are spent. And yet customers — patients, passengers, clients, citizens — still walk away without the outcome they came for. The strategy wasn’t wrong. The operating model was missing.

“Big transformations that don’t translate to the customer.” Strategy lives in slide decks. The operating model never changes.
“Teams reorganised every 18 months — same problems return.” You can’t re-org your way out of a design flaw.
“Dashboards full of green, customers full of complaints.” Metrics measure what’s easy — not what predicts Value.
“Every function doing its job, yet the outcome doesn’t arrive.” Disciplines in isolation cancel each other out.
“We need more people” — forever. Scale problems are design problems. Headcount breaks faster.
“Executive sponsorship quietly evaporates.” No line from daily activity to Value on one page.

These are not six problems. They are one problem wearing six disguises — and it has a name: the absence of an operating model built around Value.

Ready to adopt. Ready to change.
